Role and Perspective

What It Is

The same Southern Ming crisis looks different from Hongguang Emperor, Ma Shiying, Shi Kefa, a Jiangbei commander, or a low-power staff adviser. Your seat decides what you can command, whom you can only persuade, who can ignore you, and which reports arrive late.

What You Do

  1. Choose a high office with fragile survival, or a narrower command with more personal continuity.
  2. Act through office power, memorials, envoys, personal ties, and bargains.
  3. Continue the campaign through death, dismissal, exile, succession, or a changed seat.

What You See

Permission

Command previews separate direct orders, constrained requests, and ideas beyond your authority.

Perspective

You see the reports, rumors, and estimates available to your seat, not perfect world truth.

Survival

Death and office change alter your playable power instead of only showing a fail screen.

What It Is Not

  • It does not turn player historical knowledge into a supernatural ability.
  • It does not make every role a symmetric country player.
  • It does not bypass offices, armies, finance, routes, or local acceptance.
