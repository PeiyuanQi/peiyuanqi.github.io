Historical Consequences

What It Is

History is neither a fixed event tree nor random flavor. It acts as sourced pressure that changes whom people trust, whose orders armies accept, how places cooperate, and why some commands are harder than they look on paper.

What You Do

  1. Compensate, threaten, replace, or work around a command or faction with old grievances.
  2. Solve a crisis with a bargain while accepting that it may become leverage later.
  3. Read causal reports to decide whether failure came from a new problem or an old one surfacing.

What You See

Old Debts

Reports can explain resistance through enthronement, faction, arrears, failed relief, or prior orders.

Aftereffect

Your choices leave memory that shapes trust, acceptance, command fidelity, and later deals.

Flex

Historical pressure raises risk without forcing the same ending every run.

What It Is Not

  • It does not become a hard-date event tree.
  • It does not turn unsourced personality claims into hidden penalties.
  • It does not retroactively rewrite settled outcomes.
