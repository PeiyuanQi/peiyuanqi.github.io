Authority and Places

What It Is

Places are not simply yours or theirs. You may have a legal claim without tax access; an army may impose order without civil rule; and local elites can decide whether an order behaves like an order at all.

What You Do

  1. Spend scarce administrative attention on a city, crossing, or military command.
  2. Appoint officials, send inspectors, recognize military power, bargain with elites, or stabilize by force.
  3. Take short-term coercive gains while accepting long-term legitimacy and cooperation damage.

What You See

Dimensions

Places show civil, military, fiscal, legitimacy, order, and threat pressure separately.

Inertia

Control usually shifts gradually rather than flipping instantly after one order.

Cause

Reports name distance, security, elite cooperation, military pressure, or legitimacy as bottlenecks.

What It Is Not

  • It does not treat map color as rule.
  • It does not merge the Jiangbei Four Towns into one obedient or disobedient block.
  • It does not let players edit control values directly.
