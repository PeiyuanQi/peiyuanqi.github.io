Diplomacy and Promises

What It Is

Diplomacy is not a relation score. You negotiate who recognizes whom, who promises what, who can execute it, who opposes it internally, what breach would damage, and how the bargain changes legitimacy and local power.

What You Do

  1. Recognize a military command or outside power in exchange for defense, grain, silver, or time.
  2. Promise aid, request aid, propose ceasefire, open markets, or support a defection.
  3. Publicize an agreement for legitimacy, or hide it to avoid internal backlash.

What You See

Clauses

Agreements break into concrete promises instead of instant magical benefits.

Internal

Factions, commanders, gentry, merchants, and populations react differently to the same pact.

Credibility

Fulfilling, delaying, breaking, or bluffing changes future negotiation space.

What It Is Not

  • It does not replace diplomacy with one friendliness number.
  • It does not let allies ignore route, supply, and internal politics.
  • It does not make trade or recognition pure upside buttons.
