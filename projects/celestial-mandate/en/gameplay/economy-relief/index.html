Economy and Livelihood

What It Is

The economy focuses on production, consumption, transport, taxation, stockpiles, price pressure, and war priorities. A rich region may fail to feed the front; opening granaries saves lives and empties next month's reserve.

What You Do

  1. Open granaries, reserve grain for the army, borrow from salt merchants, or absorb unrest.
  2. Reduce taxes to preserve support, or increase extraction to keep armies and offices alive.
  3. Repair dikes, repair boats, secure convoys, audit ledgers, requisition grain, or restrict exports.

What You See

Shortage

Food pressure links to unrest, migration, tax compliance, legitimacy, and merchant leverage.

Stores

How much grain exists, whether it can move, and how much arrives can matter more than total output.

Debt

Merchant credit buys time while adding future fiscal and political cost.

What It Is Not

  • It does not become a full commodity market simulator.
  • It does not reduce salt, grain, silver, timber, and transport to one national resource pool.
  • It does not pull industrial gameplay into the Southern Ming demo.
