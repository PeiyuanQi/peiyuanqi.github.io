Battle and Campaigns

What It Is

Battle resolves a military operation in a particular place. It considers command, supply, morale, terrain, city defense, local cooperation, intelligence, and bounded contingency, then feeds results back into armies, places, economy, and legitimacy.

What You Do

  1. Seek a decisive clash, escort grain, hold a city, suppress disorder, or avoid battle for political room.
  2. Rush another order while the battle can still change, or authorize the commander to improvise.
  3. Accept costly victory, stalemate, withdrawal, or temporary route loss and its chain effects.

What You See

Conditions

Battle reports break down command, supply, morale, terrain, local support, and intelligence.

Chance

Rain, captured couriers, false guides, disease, and pay failure arise from existing risks.

Cost

Victory can leave fatigue, arrears, resentment, merchant leverage, or commander prestige.

What It Is Not

  • It does not explain every result with one hidden combat score.
  • It does not let random events decide outcomes without prior risk.
  • It does not simulate every background clash at the same fidelity.
