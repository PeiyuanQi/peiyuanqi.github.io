Intelligence and Uncertainty

What It Is

Intelligence makes hidden or contested reality playable. You choose what to learn, whom to trust, whether to verify, whether to protect couriers, how hard to hunt leaks, and when to act on incomplete knowledge.

What You Do

  1. Scout, bribe informants, intercept couriers, audit granaries, assess field forces, or protect your own channels.
  2. Compare merchant, staff, clerk, gentry, scout, and defector reports against each other.
  3. Increase secrecy and counterintelligence while accepting trust and false-accusation costs.

What You See

Report

Intelligence shows source class, reliability, fidelity, confidence, observation time, and arrival time.

Conflict

Two reports can both be sincere while differing in time, source, and scope.

Exposure

Spending an intelligence network can save an operation and make that channel unsafe later.

What It Is Not

  • It does not give the player full omniscience.
  • It does not become a separate assassination game.
  • It does not let reports overwrite world truth; they change what actors believe.
