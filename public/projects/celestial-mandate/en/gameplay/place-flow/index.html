Map and Routes

What It Is

Two cities are not the same distance for every purpose. Routes have capacity, safety, seasonality, local cooperation, and war disruption, turning the map into a historical network of bottlenecks.

What You Do

  1. Repair a canal, secure a ferry, reroute through the Yangtze, or negotiate passage.
  2. Treat grain, armies, messages, refugees, and rumors as different kinds of movement.
  3. Reassess what can still be reached when flood, war, or disorder breaks the usual route.

What You See

Bottleneck

The game tells you whether distance, ferries, capacity, security, season, or cooperation is blocking you.

Route

Grain may prefer water, urgent messages may prefer couriers, and armies may be slowed by wetland and supply.

Change

Geography changes with era, scenario, flood, war, and player action.

What It Is Not

  • It does not make geography only background art.
  • It does not start with an opaque full market or max-flow model.
  • It does not treat frontier or natural zones as free empty land.
