Society and Characters

What It Is

Population creates hunger, tax, migration, and unrest pressure. Special groups provide grain, silver, boats, artisans, training, reputation, and obstruction. Characters decide how orders are executed, bent, or betrayed.

What You Do

  1. Relieve refugees, placate gentry, borrow from merchants, or prioritize armies and offices.
  2. Protect a useful group, grant privilege, or suppress it and risk backlash.
  3. Appoint, reward, punish, investigate, mediate, or remove a person who can make or break execution.

What You See

Livelihood

Food prices, taxes, migration, unrest, and order gradually change local tolerance.

Leverage

Salt merchants can avert famine while demanding tax or salt concessions.

People

Loyalty, faction, competence, reputation, and ambition shape execution risk.

What It Is Not

  • It does not become a household-by-household census.
  • It does not make every profession a clickable faction.
  • It does not treat armies as ordinary social groups.
